"For the sun is no sooner risen with a burning heat, but it withereth the grass, and the flower thereof falleth, and the grace of the fashion of it perisheth: so also shall the rich man fade away in his ways."
太阳出来，热风刮起，草就枯干，花也凋谢，美容就消没了。那富足的人，在他所行的事上，也要这样衰残。

Though the received reading is ἐν ταῖς πορείαις, yet I agree with Erasmus, and read the last word, πορίαις, without the diphthong “in his riches,” or, with his riches; and the latter I prefer. (104)

(104) The received text is regarded as the best reading; the other is found in very few copies.
