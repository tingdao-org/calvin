"For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones."
因我们是他身上的肢体。（有古卷在此有就是他的骨他的肉）。

30.For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones. First, this is no exaggeration, but the simple truth. Secondly, he does not simply mean that Christ is a partaker of our nature, but expresses something higher (καὶ ἐμφατικώτερον) and more emphatic.
